About

A 36-hour FinTech hackathon of product development, where diverse ideas collide and the best ones emerge as a new product or service. It's a madhouse of creative fun, with Coding, Designing and Marketing, working together in teams for accelerated productivity. Shine in front of tech leaders presenting your project "on stage".

Vision

To Equip 1m+ Indian students with an all round Mobile Technology knowledge and implementation of Internet of Things (IoT), Ecommerce, Mcommerce and Social Media skills which will enable them to become SMART professionals.

Mission

Building ‘Digital India Hack-a-thon’ web, mobile and on the ground platform for young Indians to challenge their entrepreneurial abilities and maximize their potential in real life.


Eligibility

  • University Students. All students from any major are welcome.
  • All other Game changers, Entrepreneurs, Developers.

We welcome geeks in FinTech, InsureTech, RegTech, AI, UX designers, coders, idea generators, marketeers, data scientists, mad scientists ... You get the idea.

Registration Rules

Participants may register for this hackathon in a team of 3-5 individuals and submit ideas. These submissions must contain solutions to the Problems that are added in the Problem statement section. This is an online phase.
For the prototype submission, please add a short 2 minute elevator pitch video link while submitting. There is an option provided to do so. This would help us with a summary of your project.
Please note that all IP rights to the submissions will remain with the participants.

Problem Statements

Green & Sustainable Finance

  • How might we use technology to help funds manage their exposure to risk from unsustainable investments?
  • How can we encourage enterprises and suppliers that have not yet adopted green initiatives to do so?
  • How can we create a system that standardized sustainability reports across geographies and industries so that investors benchmark Small to medium enterprises ?
  • How can we use technology to expand ESG Instruments' distribution and liquidity?
  • How can traditional capital markets be enticed to promote green finance to SMEs?

Artificial Intelligence, IoT

  • Wearable technology - next revolution in Fin-tech: How can we leverage wearable technology opportunities in fintech? Today we can make a payment from NFC chips and given a variety of devices that are artificially intelligent and communicative with endless possibilities which brings reliability and security problems. How can we leverage wearable opportunities in banking/ payment gateway/ security/ authentication?
  • How might we leverage AI to Iot to create a single view of fraud at a customer level?
  • Build an automated Chatbot system. When a user opens the chat-box terminal, it will ask the user relevant queries and continue the conversation by asking pertinent questions in order to solve the customer’s problem

Decentralised Finance

  • Design and create a DeFi application to encourage Indians to take part in investment programmes including Green Initiatives
  • The DeFi industry has seen the introduction of interesting peer to peer finance solutions such as PoolTogether for savings, Augur for prediction markets and Compound for lending. Innovate a DeFi solution that utilises peer-to-peer finance , such as for saving , investments and loans.

Assets Class of Digital Economy

  • Almost all stakeholders have more project work to do. They frequently try to do everything by cramming more work onto already overworked project teams' calendars or by cutting corners during the project. Despite a significant investment in projects, stakeholders receive poor results because they work on the wrong projects or on too many projects.Trying to do too much leads to project delays, cost overruns, and poor quality.
  • To overcome this problem,develop a portfolio management tool that would deliver a decision making framework and a calculation engine that builds a portfolio consistent with the objective of all stakeholders (investors, risk managers , salespeople, regulators)
  • The length of the settlement period has evolved over time. For many years, the time it took to settle a trade was five days. The SEC reduced the settlement period for most securities transactions from five to three business days in 1993, then to T+2 in 2017.However, in the digital age, that three-day period appears unnecessarily long.Design a solution that would reduce the settlement process from the current 2-3 days to less than a minute
